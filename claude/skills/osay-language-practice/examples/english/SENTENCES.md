# Practice Sentences Library

Organized by topic and difficulty. Copy and use with osay.

## Daily Routines

### Beginner
- "I wake up at seven every morning."
- "I usually have coffee for breakfast."
- "I take the bus to work."
- "I eat lunch around noon."
- "I go to bed at eleven."

### Intermediate
- "I've been trying to wake up earlier lately, but it's not easy."
- "After I finish work, I like to go for a jog in the park."
- "I've gotten into the habit of reading before bed."

### Advanced
- "By the time I get home from work, I'm usually too exhausted to cook anything elaborate."
- "I've been meaning to start meditating in the mornings, but I keep hitting snooze."

## Social Interactions

### Beginner
- "Hi, how are you?"
- "Nice to meet you."
- "See you later!"
- "Thank you very much."
- "Have a good day!"

### Intermediate
- "I haven't seen you in ages! How have you been?"
- "Would you mind if I joined you?"
- "I really appreciate you taking the time to help me."

### Advanced
- "I don't mean to impose, but would it be possible to reschedule our meeting?"
- "I hate to be a bother, but could you possibly turn down the music a bit?"

## Shopping & Services

### Beginner
- "How much is this?"
- "Can I pay by card?"
- "I'm just looking, thanks."
- "Where is the restroom?"

### Intermediate
- "I'm looking for something in a medium size."
- "Do you have this in a different color?"
- "I'd like to return this, please. I have the receipt."

### Advanced
- "I was wondering if you could give me a discount since I'm buying several items."
- "The one I ordered online looked quite different from this—is there any way I could exchange it?"

## Making Plans

### Beginner
- "Are you free this weekend?"
- "Let's meet at the cafe."
- "What time works for you?"

### Intermediate
- "I was thinking we could grab dinner sometime this week."
- "How about we meet up around six, give or take?"
- "Something came up—can we push it back an hour?"

### Advanced
- "I know it's short notice, but would you be up for catching that movie tonight?"
- "Let me check my calendar and get back to you—I might have a conflict."

## Expressing Opinions

### Beginner
- "I think it's good."
- "I don't like this one."
- "This is my favorite."

### Intermediate
- "To be honest, I'm not really a fan of that idea."
- "I see what you mean, but I think there's another way to look at it."

### Advanced
- "I can see where you're coming from, but I have to respectfully disagree."
- "That's an interesting perspective—I'd never thought of it that way before."

## Phone & Messages

### Beginner
- "Hello, this is Tom speaking."
- "Can I leave a message?"
- "I'll call you back later."

### Intermediate
- "I'm sorry, I think you have the wrong number."
- "Could you speak up a bit? The connection isn't great."
- "Let me put you on hold for just a moment."

### Advanced
- "I'm returning your call from earlier—sorry I missed you."
- "I'm afraid she's stepped out—can I have her get back to you?"

## Reactions & Emotions

### Beginner
- "That's great news!"
- "I'm so happy for you."
- "That's too bad."
- "I'm sorry to hear that."

### Intermediate
- "No way! I can't believe it!"
- "That's such a relief—I was really worried."
- "I'm over the moon about this!"

### Advanced
- "I'm absolutely thrilled—this is beyond what I expected!"
- "To be perfectly honest, I'm a bit disappointed, but I understand."

## Common Idioms

### Intermediate
- "It's raining cats and dogs outside."
- "Let's play it by ear."
- "That's a piece of cake."
- "I'm feeling under the weather today."

### Advanced
- "We'll cross that bridge when we come to it."
- "You're barking up the wrong tree."
- "Let's not beat around the bush."
- "I've got a lot on my plate right now."
